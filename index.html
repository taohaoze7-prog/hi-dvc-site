Preface · 序言

Who we are.

我们是谁

In the age of artificial intelligence, traditional culture should no longer be preserved as a static archive — it should be made breathable, evolvable, and sustainable.

H&I Lab — the Heritage & Intelligence Laboratory — is the first student-founded laboratory at Diablo Valley College dedicated to the intersection of traditional Chinese culture and artificial intelligence. We are engineers and humanists, poets and product managers, builders who believe the distance between a Tang Dynasty verse and a modern language model is not as wide as it seems.

We do not study heritage as a museum piece. We let it speak again. Every retrieval pipeline, every fine-tuned model, every campus market we build is an attempt to answer a single question: what does tradition sound like when it learns the language of its own century?

Chapter II · The Three Directives

Three Pillars

Our work stands on a triad of principles — each essential, none complete without the others. Together they form the architecture of a new cultural practice.

Directive One I

Heritage

文化守望 · HERITAGE GUARDIAN

Digitally preserving the traditions that resist preservation — oral histories, ancient manuscripts, intangible heritage — before the silence becomes permanent.

Directive Two II

Intelligence

Building culture-themed AI systems — retrieval pipelines, historical agents, fine-tuned models — letting students converse across a thousand years of silence.

Chapter III · The Work

What we do.

博古通今 · 五项核心活动
i

Festive Lab

智趣节庆
Traditional festivals reimagined as AI playgrounds. Qingming, Dragon Boat, Mid-Autumn — each becomes a stage for interactive cultural experiments. Drop in, play, take home a story.
Seasonal Per Festival
ii

Culture Market

智市营生
Campus pop-up markets with 5 to 10 booths. AI-designed merchandise, traditional food, live demos. Where members learn pricing, consumer behavior, and the economics of cultural craft. Revenue splits 50% operations · 30% project teams · 20% public welfare — because sustainability is a promise we make in writing.
Twice a Semester Spring & Fall
iii

文修课

Culture Deep-Dive
Monthly lectures on traditional Chinese culture — Song aesthetics, calligraphy, ancient commerce, the poetics of classical language. Led by Secretary-General William Qiu.
Monthly 1× per Month
iv

智修课

AI Tools Workshop
Hands-on technical workshops for all majors — RAG, prompt engineering, fine-tuning, vector databases. No computer science background required. Led by President Henry Tao.
Monthly 1× per Month
v

Social Hangouts

日常交流
Low-key gatherings for boba, conversation, and connection. Because the best ideas rarely come from scheduled meetings — they come from the people you meet around a table.
Weekly As Announced
